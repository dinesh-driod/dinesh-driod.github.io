TENNIS ANALYTICS

Visualizations of player's comparison and performance in various tournaments.

INTRODUCTION

Visualization has been an important means of match analysis in a variety of sports. Like many other popular sports, tennis is increasingly defined by data. This data can be used in different ways: it can provide much-needed information about player performance for coaches, and deepen the immersion, delivering live stats, comparisons and historical perspective to enhance the fan experience for spectators. This project aims to visually show the players’ comparison and performance in various tournaments.

  • Analyze and present how ATP tennis players have performed over the years in Grand Slam Tournaments – Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon and the US Open
  • Evaluate the game over different surfaces (hard, green and clay)
  • Head-to-head against other players in various master’s series and International tournaments.
  • How Data Can Improve the Game?

Started with all players from the Association of Tennis Professionals who were active any time between the years 2009 and 2019. For those, we recorded all of their career performance data, including their yearly place in the rankings, the dates and names of all the tournaments they attended, how many score points those tournaments were worth, how many matches they played in those, against whom and what the result was.



RANKING

As expected, the 2009 period started with the dominance of Federer, Nadal and Djokovic which ended up being a consistent Top 4 as Andy Murray joined the pack.



WIN-LOSS

The biggest winner in terms of both number of wins and winning success rates in the last 11 years was Novak Djokovic with 201 wins out of 227 matches, giving him a joint top win percentage of 89% with Rafael Nadal. Nadal did win fewer matches than Roger Federer in that time period, despite a greater winning percentage; this could be explained by the injuries he sustained in 2014.



GRANDSLAM

Federer's success in Wimbledon in the late 2000s and the early 2010s still keeps him top with most Wimbledon wins, however Djokovic is on course to beat that record if he continues his form. Djokovic's Australian and US Open record show his strong hold on hard court tournaments. KEYFINDINGS

It is an unprecedented level of domination that has left little glory for other players. But who are these others and how much would have they won had they been able to overcome the Big Four?

David Ferrer, Jo Wilfried Tsonga, Tomas Berdych, Richard Gasquet and Raonic could all claim to be Slam winners.

Ferrer lost all his semi-finals and a final to the Big Four, and winning a Slam would have been a fitting reward for his many years of elite-level performance.

One player who may deserve to be even higher on the list is del Potro, who had to face a member of the Big Four in every semi-final he played and never went beyond the quarter-finals at the Australian Open, twice knocked out by Federer.

What Matters Most to Winning?

Serve percentage.

On the ATP tour from 2010 to 2013, when a player made exactly half of his first serves, he won 42.8% of the time. At 60% first serves in, he won 47.0% of the time. At 70%, the winning percentage is 57.4%.
